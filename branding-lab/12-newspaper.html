Local Mind Finally Finds Where It Put That Thought

New block-based workspace nests folders, documents & ideas into one tidy tree; experts call it "the obvious thing we should've had all along."
By the Editorial Board · Filed under Everything

In a development being hailed across every desk of this newspaper, Nexus has reportedly ended the centuries-old practice of losing one's own notes between scattered apps, forgotten folders, and the backs of envelopes.

The system, sources confirm, treats every item — a folder, a document, even a calendar entry — as a single kind of object: a node. These nodes nest inside one another without limit, forming a tree that can be rearranged with a single drag.

Documents themselves are assembled from ordered blocks — headings, paragraphs, images, video, embeds — which may be reshuffled at will. Witnesses describe the experience as "suspiciously pleasant."

Collaborators reportedly see one another's cursors move in real time, while a built-in importer is said to ingest pages from rival publications and re-set them in clean Nexus type.
