# 🎨 Nexus — Art Direction Bible

Six worlds. Six studios that hate each other. One product: **Nexus**, a
block-based knowledge workspace — folders contain documents contain blocks,
infinitely nested, with a content calendar, real-time editing, and Notion
import. Every world is a *metaphor for the product*, never a skin.

---

## 01 — The Flash Drive / Desktop OS  →  `01-flashdrive-os.html`

- **Concept:** *Nexus as your memory, externalized — a 2004 operating system you boot into.*
- **Mood / refs:** Windows XP Luna, Mac OS Aqua, iPod click-wheel plastic, Y2K translucent hardware, the satisfying *clunk* of a USB drive.
- **Fonts:** Tahoma / "Trebuchet MS" UI + a pixel display face (Press Start 2P for accents).
- **Palette:** Bliss-hill teal `#3a6ea5`, plastic silver `#ece9d8`, aqua glass `#5ba4e6`, taskbar blue gradient `#245edb → #3f8cf3`, lime go-button `#73d216`.
- **Signature interaction:** A chunky translucent USB drive in the hero that **fills with capacity as you scroll** (0% → 100%), plus draggable desktop icons and a working Start menu.
- **Headline:** "Insert your brain." · **CTA:** "▶ Run nexus.exe"
- **Why it fits:** A flash drive *is* externalized memory and nested folders — the most literal possible model of Nexus's node tree.

## 02 — Olympus, the Library of the Gods  →  `02-olympus-archive.html`

- **Concept:** *Nexus as the Archive of Mnemosyne — a hierarchy of gods is a tree of nodes.*
- **Mood / refs:** Marble and gold leaf, fluted Doric columns, Greek meander borders, constellation ceilings, carved tablets, papyrus.
- **Fonts:** Cinzel (carved display) + Cormorant Garamond (body).
- **Palette:** Pentelic marble `#f4f1ea`, deep aegean `#1b2a4a`, gold leaf `#c9a14a`, oxidized bronze `#5e6b54`, ink `#2a2620`.
- **Signature interaction:** A vertical "scroll" that unrolls (papyrus reveal) + parallax columns + a constellation/lineage map of the pantheon = the node tree.
- **Headline:** "Every thought, made immortal." · **CTA:** "Enter the Archive"
- **Why it fits:** Mnemosyne is memory itself; Olympus's divine lineage is literally a parent→child tree — Nexus's data model rendered as myth.

## 03 — Brutalist Terminal / Hacker Archive  →  `03-terminal-brutalist.html`

- **Concept:** *Nexus as raw infrastructure — a system you command, not an app you click.*
- **Mood / refs:** VT220 phosphor, `htop`, dotfiles, raw HTML 1.0, man pages, ASCII art.
- **Fonts:** IBM Plex Mono everywhere. No exceptions.
- **Palette:** True black `#0a0a0a`, phosphor green `#33ff66`, amber alt `#ffb000`, dim grid `#1a1a1a`, alert red `#ff3b3b`.
- **Signature interaction:** A **working fake CLI** in the hero — type `nexus tree`, `nexus init`, `help` and get real responses; streaming system logs; ASCII node-tree diagram.
- **Headline:** "> nexus init" · **CTA:** "$ npm i -g nexus"
- **Why it fits:** The node tree is literally a filesystem tree; the terminal renders Nexus's structure in its native, unadorned form.

## 04 — The Knowledge Constellation  →  `04-cosmic-constellation.html`

- **Concept:** *Nexus as a personal cosmos — notes are stars, folders are galaxies, links are gravity.*
- **Mood / refs:** Deep-space photography, planetarium, aurora, star charts, weightlessness.
- **Fonts:** Fraunces (optical display) + Spline Sans / system for body.
- **Palette:** Void `#05060f`, nebula violet `#6d5bd0`, plasma cyan `#33e0ff`, star white `#f4f7ff`, ember `#ff7a59`.
- **Signature interaction:** A **draggable / drifting node-graph constellation** on `<canvas>` — drag stars, they're wired by gravity lines; parallax starfield; everything orbits.
- **Headline:** "Map the universe in your head." · **CTA:** "Chart your first star"
- **Why it fits:** A knowledge graph *is* a constellation — discrete points made meaningful only by the lines between them.

## 05 — The Detective's Evidence Board  →  `05-evidence-board.html`

- **Concept:** *Nexus as the case wall — everything is connected, and here's the red string proving it.*
- **Mood / refs:** Cork board, red yarn, index cards, polaroids, typewriter, rubber stamps, washi tape, coffee rings.
- **Fonts:** Special Elite (typewriter) + Patrick Hand (marker) + Courier accents.
- **Palette:** Cork `#c8a165`, manila `#f3e7c9`, evidence red `#c4302b`, stamp blue ink `#2b3a67`, graphite `#33312e`.
- **Signature interaction:** **Red string that draws itself** between pinned cards (animated SVG paths), cards pinned at slight rotations, drag-to-nudge, stamp-thunk on CTA.
- **Headline:** "Connect the dots in your own head." · **CTA:** "Open the case file"
- **Why it fits:** Nexus links blocks and documents across a tree — the evidence board is "everything is connected" made physical with string.

## 06 — The Mycelial Brain  →  `06-mycelial-brain.html`

- **Concept:** *Nexus as a living thing — a second brain that grows, not a tool you fill.*
- **Mood / refs:** Mycelium networks, neurons firing, bioluminescent fungi, root systems, slime-mold maps, breathing organisms.
- **Fonts:** Unbounded (organic display) + Hanken Grotesk (body).
- **Palette:** Spore black `#0c0f0d`, bioluminescent teal `#28ffd0`, fungal magenta `#ff4fd8`, moss `#2f6b4f`, bone `#eef2ec`.
- **Signature interaction:** A **tree/mycelium that grows nodes as you scroll** (animated SVG growth), pulsing/breathing gradients, blobby morphing shapes, synaptic firing lines.
- **Headline:** "Grow a second brain." · **CTA:** "Plant the first node"
- **Why it fits:** Nexus nests nodes infinitely and links them — biological growth is the most honest metaphor for a knowledge base that compounds.

# ROUND TWO — the new six

> Feedback: the client kept **03 (Terminal)** and **05 (Evidence Board)** and asked for
> six more. Read taste = two veins: **raw / technical / interactive** and
> **tactile / analog real-object**. The new six live in those veins (plus a couple
> of wild cards), and avoid the glossy/gradient registers that didn't land.

## 08 — The Card Catalog  →  `08-card-catalog.html`  *(tactile/analog — sibling of 05)*
- **Concept:** *Every node a typed card, every folder a drawer you slide open.*
- **Fonts:** DM Serif Display + Crimson Pro + Cutive Mono (catalog cards).
- **Palette:** oak `#6b4f2e`, card cream `#f5ecd7`, library red `#8a2f2a`, stamp purple `#4a3b6b`, brass `#b08d4f`.
- **Signature:** wooden cabinet whose **drawers click open** to reveal typed catalog cards; Dewey-decimal call numbers; due-date stamps as the calendar.
- **Headline:** "Every thought, catalogued." · **CTA:** "Apply for a card"
- **Why it fits:** the card catalog is the most beautiful tree-index ever built — Nexus's hierarchy, by hand.

## 09 — The Blueprint  →  `09-blueprint.html`  *(technical-tactile)*
- **Concept:** *Your knowledge, drawn to scale.*
- **Fonts:** Saira Condensed + Anonymous Pro.
- **Palette:** cyanotype blue `#0b3d91`, line cyan `#7fb0e8`, ink white `#eaf2ff`, amber `#ffd56b`, redline `#ff5a5a`.
- **Signature:** drafting grid, the tree as a **dimensioned floor plan / axonometric**, spec-table features, a Gantt construction schedule, title-block CTA. Hover highlights elements.
- **Headline:** "Knowledge, drawn to scale." · **CTA:** "Approve for build"
- **Why it fits:** a workspace is a structure; nesting is architecture; a plan makes it sound.

## 10 — The Transit Map  →  `10-transit-map.html`  *(systemic-diagrammatic; structural clarity of 03 in light form)*
- **Concept:** *Documents are stations, folders interchanges, links the lines.*
- **Fonts:** Familjen Grotesk + DM Mono.
- **Palette:** map paper `#f5f2e9`, ink `#1c1c1c`, TfL-ish line colors (red/blue/green/gold/purple/teal).
- **Signature:** an SVG **metro map whose colored route lines draw themselves**; roundel logo; "you are here"; a live departures-board calendar.
- **Headline:** "Mind the gap in your knowledge." · **CTA:** "Buy a ticket"
- **Why it fits:** a knowledge graph routed like a network — every thought reachable, no dead ends.

## 11 — The Modular Synth  →  `11-modular-synth.html`  *(interactive + tactile — patch cables echo 05's string)*
- **Concept:** *Patch your thoughts together and hear what they make.*
- **Fonts:** Syne + DM Mono.
- **Palette:** rack charcoal `#16161a`, eurorack panel `#d8d2c4`, amber `#ff8a1e`, patch-cable yellow/blue/red, LED green.
- **Signature:** a hardware module with knobs, jacks, VU meter, and **patch cables that draw themselves** (bezier SVG) between ports; tree as signal flow; a step-sequencer calendar.
- **Headline:** "Patch your mind." · **CTA:** "Power on"
- **Why it fits:** modular = re-patchable structure; the signal flows parent→child exactly like the node tree.

## 12 — The Daily Nexus (Newspaper)  →  `12-newspaper.html`  *(analog editorial — wild card)*
- **Concept:** *All the knowledge that's fit to nest.*
- **Fonts:** UnifrakturCook (masthead) + Playfair Display (headlines) + Spectral / Old Standard TT (body).
- **Palette:** newsprint `#efe9dc`, ink `#1a1714`, spot red `#b3261e`, halftone gray.
- **Signature:** real broadsheet layout — multi-column justified story, drop cap, halftone cut, the tree as a "family tree," features as classifieds, calendar as the publishing agenda.
- **Headline:** "Local Mind Finally Finds Where It Put That Thought." · **CTA:** "Subscribe to your own mind"
- **Why it fits:** a newspaper is a nested hierarchy of sections, desks and columns — print's version of the node tree.

## 13 — NEXUS.EXE (DOS Commander)  →  `13-dos-commander.html`  *(raw system — sibling of 03, blue text-mode)*
- **Concept:** *The file commander that shipped on a floppy — no mouse needed.*
- **Fonts:** VT323 (pixel CRT) everywhere.
- **Palette:** DOS blue `#0000a8`, cyan `#00a8a8`/`#54fcfc`, white, yellow `#fcfc54`, green/red accents.
- **Signature:** Norton-Commander **dual-pane file manager** (arrow-key navigable), ANSI box-drawing borders, a bottom **F-key function bar**, blinking block cursor, batch-scheduler calendar.
- **Headline:** "Your brain, commanded." · **CTA:** "F2 — Run NEXUS.EXE"
- **Why it fits:** the node tree is a filesystem; a dual-pane commander is the rawest way to move nodes between branches.

# ROUND THREE — Modern / Awwwards

> Direction change: none of the themed concepts was "the one." Client asked for a
> **modern, Awwwards-style** register instead. Six contemporary award-site looks,
> each distinct, high-craft, anti-slop (no purple-on-white).

## 14 — Swiss Editorial  →  `14-swiss-editorial.html`  *(light)*
- **Concept:** *International typographic style — knowledge as a strict, broken grid.*
- **Fonts:** Anton (heavy display) + Archivo. **Palette:** paper `#f1efe8`, ink `#0c0c0c`, electric red `#ff2d16`.
- **Signature:** mix-blend-difference nav, oversized uppercase hero with outline type, **kinetic marquee**, numbered editorial index for the tree, intentional grid-breaks.

## 15 — Kinetic Brutalist  →  `15-kinetic-brutalist.html`  *(dark)*
- **Concept:** *Loud structure for a quiet mind.*
- **Fonts:** Bricolage Grotesque + DM Mono. **Palette:** near-black `#0b0b0b`, acid-lime `#d4ff00`.
- **Signature:** giant type-driven hero, italic + outline mix, **magnetic buttons**, marquee strip, hover-invert oversized tree rows.

## 16 — Editorial Serif  →  `16-editorial-serif.html`  *(light, luxe)*
- **Concept:** *Less like software, more like a well-kept library.*
- **Fonts:** Fraunces (optical serif) + Hanken Grotesk. **Palette:** cream `#f6f3ec`, ink `#16140f`, clay `#b5613f`.
- **Signature:** quiet fashion-mag whitespace, big italic display, full-bleed statement quote, refined contents list, thin-rule micro-interactions.

## 17 — Neo-Brutalism  →  `17-neo-brutalism.html`  *(light, playful)*
- **Concept:** *Snap your brain into chunky, colorful blocks.*
- **Fonts:** Archivo Black + Space Grotesk. **Palette:** sunny `#fdf3d8`, ink, electric blue/pink/yellow/green/orange.
- **Signature:** 3px borders, hard offset shadows, rotated stickers, press-down buttons, a live "block stack" card, colorful nested tree.

## 18 — Bento Product  →  `18-bento-product.html`  *(dark, SaaS)*
- **Concept:** *The workspace that thinks in blocks — a polished product page.*
- **Fonts:** Hanken Grotesk. **Palette:** ink `#08090c`, periwinkle `#7c8cff`, mint `#5ee7c2`.
- **Signature:** Linear/Apple-style **bento grid** with hover glow, floating pill nav, mini editor/tree/avatar/calendar mocks inside cells, gradient-glow CTA.

## 19 — Mono Interactive  →  `19-mono-interactive.html`  *(black & white)*
- **Concept:** *Structure for the mind — agency-of-the-year minimalism.*
- **Fonts:** Syne + DM Mono. **Palette:** black `#0a0a0a`, off-white `#f4f4f2`, grain overlay.
- **Signature:** **custom blend-mode cursor** that scales + labels on hover, **magnetic CTA**, **drag-scroll horizontal** feature gallery, hover-invert tree rows, grain.

## Shared rules honored across all six
- Distinct type system, color world, layout logic, signature interaction, texture, and in-world copy voice per variant.
- ≥2 variants abandon vertical scroll as the primary logic (OS windows, constellation canvas).
- Real Nexus capabilities as content (node tree, blocks, calendar, real-time, Notion import) — translated into each world's language.
- No purple-on-white gradients, no 3 identical rounded cards, no "Empower your workflow."
